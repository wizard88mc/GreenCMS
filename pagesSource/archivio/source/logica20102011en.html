Contents

1. Language, sign and expression, symbol and proposition, assertion and declaration, metalanguage, reference levels, infinite iteration.

2. Notion of machine or robot, meaning of connectives and their deductive rules, classical logic (intuitionistic logic will also be mentioned), truth tables, propositional functions and subsets, quantifiers and their deductive rules.

3. Decision methods for classical propositional sequent calculi.

4. Definition and proof by induction, term and formula, interpretation of formulas, notion of model and contramodel, validity, satisfability and unsatisfability of a formula.

5. Sketch of completeness and incompleteness (Goedel) theorems and (Church) indecidability theorem with discussion on their meaning.

6. Detailed analysis of a concrete example: axiomatic theory of Peano Arithmetics
